## Problem (one paragraph)

10-hour HVAC control for a 2-room restaurant. Per hour pick `p1, p2 ∈ [0, 3] kW` (continuous) and `V ∈ {0,1}` (binary). Cost = `price_t · (p1 + p2 + p_vent · V)`, summed over t=0..9. Exogenous stochastic: electricity price (mean-reverting AR(2) in `processes/PriceProcessRestaurant.py`) and per-room occupancy (Markovian AR in `processes/OccupancyProcessRestaurant.py`). Endogenous state evolves linearly in T1, T2, H from the chosen actions and exogenous occupancy. Three overrules are enforced by the simulator: high-temp (heater forced off above 26°C), low-temp (heater forced to max once T<18, until T≥22), humidity (V forced on above 70%), plus ventilation inertia (once V=1 it stays on ≥3 hours). Action time budget per call: **15 s** (else dummy action is substituted by `check_and_sanitize_action`).

## State the policy receives

`{T1, T2, H, Occ1, Occ2, price_t, price_previous, vent_counter, low_override_r1, low_override_r2, current_time}`. `current_time ∈ [0, 9]`. Initial state from `SystemCharacteristics.get_fixed_data()`: `T1 = T2 = 21.0`, `H = 40.0`, `vc = lo1 = lo2 = 0`.

## The central analytical finding (writeup section "When does SP fail")

Across 100 days, the per-day SP regret over hindsight has mean 11.1 and median 5.5 — concentrated in a tail of ~20 days. Correlating regret with 14 candidate per-day measures:
- `price_trend` (`p_9 − p_0`): **r = +0.72** (dominant signal).
- `ar1_surprise` (Σ squared 1-step AR(2) residuals): r = −0.02 (essentially zero).
- `mean price`, `hindsight cost`: small negative.

**Mechanism**: the price model is mean-reverting AR(2) with long-run mean 4. On rising-price days (`p_0 ≈ 3`, `p_9 ≈ 7`), SP's scenario tree at t=0 samples late prices that regress toward 4 — a late-day spike doesn't appear in any cluster center. Hindsight, knowing the spike is coming, pre-heats hard during the cheap morning. SP heats uniformly and pays for the expensive afternoon.

**Why no leaf augmentation we tried helps**: the SP-trained V_θ has no trend feature in its 11-dim φ; CFA penalties are on leaf *state* not future *trajectory*; the MPC deterministic tail forecasts roll the same mean-reverting model; deeper trees still sample from the same distribution. **The bottleneck is the uncertainty model, not the policy class.**

## Key gotchas

- **Actions are mixed** (`V` is binary with a 3-step lockout). Any continuous-action method needs special handling for `V`.
- **Comfort constraints are enforced post-hoc by the simulator**; the override controllers are essentially automatic recourse. Trying to anticipate them with CFA penalties usually causes over-spending on preemptive heating (see CFA grid search result).
- `outdoor_temperature` is deterministic from the config; only price and occupancy are stochastic.
- **Hindsight MILP initial state** must be `T1=T2=21, H=40` matching `SystemCharacteristics.get_fixed_data()`. The humidity dynamics in `experiments/hindsight_optimal.py` use `>=` (not `==`) to mirror the simulator's `np.clip(H, 0, 100)`.
- **The MPC tail's chained-mean forecast is action-independent** — it only works because the exogenous processes (price, occupancy) don't depend on actions. For an action-dependent exogenous process you'd need an ADP-style value function instead.
- **Simulator's `something_crazy` warning** is from a self-test in `helper/v2_Checks.py` and is unrelated to the policy under test; always present in eval logs.
- **`policies/sp_policy.py` is the working reference** — don't modify it.
